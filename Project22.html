My Cookies

What Are Cookies?

Cookies are small text files saved in your browser by websites to store data about you.

  • Session Cookies – Temporary, disappear after closing browser.
  • Persistent Cookies – Stored for a set time, like login or preferences.
  • Third-Party Cookies – Created by external domains (e.g. ads).
  • Secure Cookies – Transmitted only over HTTPS.
  • HttpOnly Cookies – Inaccessible to JavaScript, used for security.

Why They Matter:

Cookies help websites remember you, but they can also be used for tracking. Understanding your cookies is the first step to better privacy.

Managing Cookies:

  • You can delete cookies through your browser settings.
  • In Chrome: Settings > Privacy and security > Cookies and other site data > See all cookies and site data
  • In Firefox: Settings > Privacy & Security > Cookies and Site Data > Manage Data
  • In Safari: Preferences > Privacy > Manage Website Data

Privacy Tips:

  • Use incognito/private browsing to avoid storing cookies.
  • Install browser extensions that block tracking cookies (e.g. uBlock, Privacy Badger).
  • Regularly clear your cookies and site data.
