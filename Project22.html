What Are Cookies?

Cookies are small text files stored by your browser when you visit websites. They are used to remember you, your preferences, or your activity on the website. Cookies are essential for many modern web features such as logins, shopping carts, language preferences, and analytics.

  • Session Cookies: Exist temporarily and are deleted once the browser is closed. Used for things like keeping you logged in during a session.
  • Persistent Cookies: Remain on your device for a set time, often used for remembering login data or preferences between sessions.
  • Third-Party Cookies: Placed by domains other than the one youвЂ™re visiting (e.g., advertising and tracking cookies from external services).
  • Secure Cookies: Can only be transmitted over HTTPS connections. Protect data during transmission.
  • HttpOnly Cookies: Not accessible by JavaScript. Used to protect sensitive information such as session tokens.
